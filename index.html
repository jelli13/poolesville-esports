2026 Varsity Team

Qualifications

Varsity (5 players max): You must meet all the same qualifications as athletics:

  • No major discipline issues
  • Practices are mandatory to play (unless advance notice is given)
  • You cannot have an unexcused absence the day of a game
  • You must be ranked at least Platinum I by the end of Week 2 of competition (negotiable if making significant progress)
  • Jerseys required for Captain, Assistant Captain, and Anchor (optional for subs and JV)

What is Esports?

Poolesville High School Esports is part of a larger county-level Esports league called the Montgomery County Esports League (MCEL). The Montgomery County Esports League (MCEL) was founded in 2023 as a league of ten schools across the county that compete in a 7-week season that ends with a championship series to determine the league champion.

What is Esports and how is it different from casual gaming?

If you make the varsity Esports roster, it requires the same level of dedication and teamwork expected from players of a physical sport. If you make the junior varsity roster, you are working towards earning a varsity roster spot and supporting the program. Instead of physical acumen, the sport requires fine motor skills, mental toughness, creativity, communication, and collaboration.

Why Esports? What can I get out of it?

Over 200 colleges and universities belong to the National Association of Collegiate Esports, have a collegiate esports team, and give scholarships ranging from $500 to $10,000 (the average is $2,000). Professional esports teams compete for six-figure prize pools. For example, participants in the 2024 Rocket League Championship Series played for a share of a $1.16 million prize pool with the winner (Team BDS) walking away with a $300,000 grand prize.
